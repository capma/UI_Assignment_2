Chapter 07: Designing the home page

« Previous Next »

The most important task, and the thing that most often gets lost, for the homepage is conveying the big picture. As clearly as possible the homepage needs to answer these 4 questions:

  1. What is this?

  2. What do they have here?

  3. What can I do here?

  4. Why should I be here - and not somewhere else?

Users need to be able to answer those questions, rather than on trying to impress, entice, or directing the user to your promotions.


The top 5 plausible excuses for not spelling out the big picture on the homepage are:

  1. We don't need to, it is so obvious.

  2. After people have seen the explanation they won't be offended by seeing the same explanation.

  3. Anyone who really needs our site will know what it is.

  4. That's what our advertising is for.

  5. We'll just add a "New to this site?" link.


How to get the message across:

  • There should be a tagline next to the Site ID. The content should be clear and informative, convey differentiation and a clear benefit.

  • The welcome blurb: a crisp description of the site, displayed prominently.
