Chapter 03: A Plan You Can Actually Follow

« Previous Next »

Most people don’t do the usability testing is because they think it has to be a big project

In fact, it’s doable and it helps you to focus on finding and fixing the most important problems first.

So the suggested plan for the usability testing should be: a morning a month

Here is an overview of the differences between Big Project Test and Do-It-Yourself testing:

THE BIG PROJECT TEST DO-IT-YOURSELF TESTING
Time spent for each round of testing 1 day for designing test cases then 1–2 days of tests, then a week to prepare a briefing, depends on process to decide what to fix One morning a month includes designing test scripts, testing, debriefing, and deciding what to fix
When does the test start? When the site is almost done Continually, throughout the process of website development
Number of rounds of testing Only one or two per project, because of time and expense One morning every month
Number of testers in each round 5–8 members, sometimes 10 3 members
