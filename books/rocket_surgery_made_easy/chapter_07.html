Chapter 07: Why You Should Use Boring Checklists

« Previous Next »

On the day of testing, you should have a checklist with all the details that are going to happen so you can be more relaxed and give your full attention to the participant.


For example:

Three weeks before

  • Imagine what you’re going to be testing

  • Come up with a list of tasks to test


Two weeks before

  • Get feedback from the team members and stakeholders on your list

  • Making a schedule for the participants


One week before

  • Send an email to the participants with full directions like parking instructions, the location of the test room, name and phone number of someone to call on the test day if they’re late or lost...


One or two days before

  • Reconfirm with participants by calling and asking if they have any questions

  • Inform observers by email


Test day (before the first test)

  • Ask observer to be in the observation room

  • Make sure the website is working on the test computer or the wifi is working
