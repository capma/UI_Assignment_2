Chapter 10: Comparing Notes And Deciding What To Fix

« Previous

When you come out of the room, make sure you have two lists in your hand:

  • A list of the most critical usability problems that participants encountered during the test

  • A list of the problems that you're going to fix before next testing

Which problems should be fixed?

  • You have to be extremely focused on fixing the most critical problems first.

Decide which problems are the most critical

  • Will the problem being experienced by a lot of people?

  • Will it cause a serious problem to the end user?

In the debriefing meeting

  • Ask everyone to review and choose the three most serious problems that they wrote down during the test session.

  • From the list of problems provided by everyone, look and choose what seems to be the ten most serious.

  • Order these top ten problems by serious.

  • Ask the team discuss how each problem can be fixed within the next month.

  • Continue working on the list until you think that all the resources you have available for fixing things in the next month, then stop.
